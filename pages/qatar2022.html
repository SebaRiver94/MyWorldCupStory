Stadiums

Qatar's World Cup stadiums blended cutting-edge design with strong cultural references, each one telling a story through its architecture — from patterns inspired by traditional Arab art to structures shaped like iconic regional symbols. Watching games there felt modern, comfortable, and incredibly intense, with world-class facilities, perfect pitch conditions, and immersive fan experiences. Every match became more than just football; it was a cultural showcase, a celebration of innovation, and a perfect stage for unforgettable moments on and off the field.

Matches I attended

Matchdays in Qatar were pure electricity. The short travel between venues meant more time to share with fans from every corner of the world, turning each day into a continuous celebration. From the shocking opener against Saudi Arabia, to the high-pressure clash with Mexico, and the knockout battle against Australia, every match carried its own drama and passion. Inside the stadiums, chants and drums echoed in every direction, while outside, streets and fan zones became vibrant festivals of color, culture, and unbreakable football spirit.

Trip Summary

Qatar 2022 felt unique: short distances between stadiums made moving around effortless, while the futuristic venues amazed with their design and atmosphere. The entire city lived and breathed football for a month — streets decorated with flags, fans from all over the world sharing songs, and an unmatched festive energy day and night. It was the perfect mix of travel, culture, and unforgettable football moments, enriched by the warmth of the locals, diverse culinary experiences, and the charm of exploring a modern city with deep-rooted traditions.
